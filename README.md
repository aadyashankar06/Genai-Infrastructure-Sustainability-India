# India's GenAI Infrastructure Crisis – Sustainability Framework

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes the hidden environmental and economic costs of India's rapidly growing GenAI infrastructure and proposes a structured sustainability framework to align economic incentives with environmental accountability.

## 🚨 Problem Statement
- GenAI growth is driving massive increases in energy and water consumption  
- Environmental costs remain unpriced (externalities)  
- Current efficiency metrics (like PUE) fail to capture real impact  
- Leads to unsustainable and misleading infrastructure expansion  

## 💡 Key Insights
- GenAI workloads require 7–8x more power than traditional workloads  
- Data centers consume massive water resources (cooling vs agriculture conflict)  
- 70% of India's electricity grid relies on coal  
- Current metrics enable "greenwashing" and hide true environmental cost  

## 🧠 Approach
- Identified core failure:
  → Externalities are unpriced → market distortion  

- Designed **USI (Unified Sustainability Index)**:
  - Energy (E) – 40% weight  
  - Water (W) – 30% weight  
  - Carbon (C) – 30% weight  

- Proposed:
  - Carbon pricing mechanism  
  - Water pricing based on regional stress  
  - Incentives for renewable transition  
  - Regulatory reforms for transparency  

## 📊 Impact
- Aligns private profit with environmental sustainability  
- Reduces coal dependency and emissions  
- Enables scalable green AI infrastructure  
- Positions India as a global leader in sustainable AI
